Miss Behaves
Cece Natalie

Late last year, scrolling instagram idly, I happened to hear a tiny snippet of a great song over a forgettable meme. Although the song wasn't listed - I heard "I don't drive anybody 'round, like a taxi", so I hunted down "Like a taxi". Then I listened to Ambulance. Then I listened to 754. And then I came to love the entire album. Although she's not very well known, Cece Natalie is an impressive artist: a self-produced, driven musician, making fantastic music that's still flying under the radar. Miss Behaves is her debut album, and it's simply one of the most effective pop albums I've ever heard - showing its pop, scenecore, and clashy DNA while pushing the envelope enough to be raucously enjoyable from end-to-end. 

I think the obvious touch point for why pop music is good is that it's catchy, and god damn is this album catchy. I love the production throughout this album - it impressively strikes the balance between accessible, catchy beats and engaging, genre-fusing innovation. Nearly every song has a hook or chorus that gets stuck in my head, even months after first listen. On both versions of 754 !SM, the synergy between the beat and her vocals is immense. Her airy vocals soar over the building instrumentation, to great effect. I particularly love the refrain, with each syllable of "The right amount of nothing, but you got a perfect timing" matching a hit of the melody or percussion. Perfect timing indeed. Another immensely catchy jam: Team Cece !SM, with an exceptional chorus that has been utterly embossed in my brain for months. Just the "All the players on Team Cece" atop the claps and whistle blowing was enough to lodge this song in my head. This is supported by the structure: "Let's sweat, let's sweat, let's get // Zero feet apart" - this auditory enjambment perfectly fits the lyrics - dragging lines across their natural breaks reinforces getting 'zero feet apart'. The bouncy, straightforward beat under "Let's walk // a little, let's break the ice" has a natural step to the rhythm, again matching the content. Moreover, I'm particularly charmed by the choice of the whistle. Though I think the obvious touchpoint for scene-pop trappings is a camera shutter snippet, the whistle fits right in. Very fitting for the queen of Team Cece - and a nice callback to an earlier era of pop music. 

On the theme of where Cece fits into landscape of pop, I really appreciate her niche. I've written at length about my love for Maude Latour's music - it's vibrant, full of love for the world, shamelessly bright - and very fitting for warm, summery pop beats. This album is the exact opposite: self-centered, irreverent, hedonistic and uncompromising - and I love it all the same. Although I often find sexual overtones to be distasteful, I'm surprised by how well they work here. Considering the young, free-spirit, eponymous misbehaving persona she's building, the lyrics are effective. On the second verse Tennis Court !SM, for example: "Nineteen, horny, and I'm broke". Direct, but effective atop the slow, pulsing bass and rhythmic claps. Continuing into the chorus - "I never did this before, yeah, but you need it now" - is particularly catchy. Similarly, on Oh Oh !SM, she builds the self-centered personality: "I do not listen to this shit, I am too feminine for it // I can't name anybody here, cuz it's music for boys". Uncaring, maybe even disrespectful, but very charming. And of course, "Oh Oh" means the same as Ex's and Oh's: "Can you make me oh oh, I don't care, I'm coming over". These are just a few examples - on nearly every track, she embodies a "Miss Behaves". In a world where so many pop stars cater to the lowest-common-denominator sound, I find it refreshing how clear her strong personality comes through vibrantly throughout the album.

Although it is generally excellent and well-positioned, there are times where her sound feels unrefined, particularly on the bookends of the album. The opening track, Limit !SM, hasn't had the same sticking power as the later tracks. I find the "la la la" to be a bit trite, and the overall song less compelling than the rest of the album. It improves as the track goes on, but it's still a bit lackluster in comparison to the heavy hitters like 754. Though it's still a decent track, in comparison to the rest of the tracklist, it's a bit bland. At the other end, Gymnastics !SM - a good idea, lacking on the execution. Although I like the rapid, layering lyrics, they're lost in the mix - overpowered by the other elements. The horn is too sharp, her vocals too sibilant, and the overall mix is a bit thin. It's an enjoyable track for sure - but the danceable vocal chops don't fully overcome the issues for me. I wish this song, not 754, got an alternate version, as a refined mix could make for a great track. In lieu of that, we're left with a disappointing showing for the closer track. Still, the quality of nearly element is high throughout the body of the album, combining strong writing, instrumentation, percussion, and an undeniable presence from Cece Natalie. 

There's one track that combines all these points - Like a taxi !SM. From the very first listen, I knew this song was something special. The production feels very spacious and chilling: with straightforward percussion, distant echoing vocals, and the voice/synth combo filling the top end. I find that this sound perfectly platforms the nonchalant, taunting lyrics: "I know you couldn't handle me // But I still made a cameo in all your wild dreams (Oh well)" and "Might just forget about the things you thought you knew about me". Super effective - oozing hot-girl cool that's very charming as a listener. Beyond their content, the lyrics also sound amazing. I find the internal rhythm particularly euphonic - the dual hits of "Dive in me; I really - make it crazy". Continuing onto the next line, the "Don't *die for me*" matching perfectly atop the buzzing melody makes quite a nice sound. The melody on its own is also extremely strong, textured enough to remain interesting, without taking over the vocals, or becoming irritating on repeated listens. It's an ideal undercurrent for the chorus, and fills the soundstage seamlessly when her vocals drop away. Finally, after the repeated "oh wells", there's a quick, airy riser, like a gust of wind, that brings the melody back with the chorus. Though it's a microscopic detail - present for under a second - it is a seamless transitions, and clearly demonstrates the flawless arrangement of this song. Between the handful At just under three minutes, this track is exemplary of the album's best qualities: tight, well-produced, sassy, and undeniably catchy. 

Yes, Cece Natalie isn't yet well known - but I've shown you why she absolutely should be. Strong songwriting, amazing production, and a nicely idiosyncratic blend of new and old pop sounds. In the handful of months since discovering this album, I just can't get it out of my head! I keep returning to 754, Team Cece, Lovely, and Like a taxi, and looping them for hours at a time. Meanwhile, she continues to release new music, and continue refining her sound. Seeing that she's touring with PinkPantheress later this year, I'm excited that 2026 might really be Cece's year. Whether she sees mainstream success or not, I'll be listening. I hope that she continues releasing new music, exploring new sounds, and finding the success she so obviously deserves.
