Night Killas Vol. 1
Snow Strippers

Across the past two reviews, we've discussed the range of Snow Strippers' sound: So What If I'm A Freak's indelible groove and high replayability; Stab the Voice's dark and otherworldly sound. With this established, let's expand the scope, and discuss Night Killas Vol. 1: a tight, 16-minute collection of some of the best tracks they've made.

At under twenty minutes in length across seven tracks, this EP doesn't overstay its welcome, even for a second. It feels that there's an energy, a freneticism to the tracks here, and as a listener, I find this absolutely contagious. Consider the opener - Aching Like It's !SM - four-on-the-floor kicks, Tati's voice interwoven with the sample, and endless movement in the instrumental. The constant rising and falling, panning audible air, and repeated "Aching Like It's-" all just make me want to get moving. Cautious !SM is even more frenetic - the pitched-up vocals, rapid lyrics, and near out-of-breath delivery all packed into a 93-second runtime. It feels as if she can barely get the words out, like a rushed confession "If you tell me the truth // It'd be useless at this point // I made it all- I made it all up". This delivery, surrounded by sharp inhales and atop a literal siren sample, imparts a real urgency to the track. It is impossible to be bored whilst listening to these tracks. 

Without a throughline, these tracks would feel messy and overwhelming. For this, each track has a clear, catchy anchor; something to latch onto and sing along with. On Just Your Doll !SM, the pre-chorus is electric: "Break my fall // Please don't let my veins constrict // I'm Just Your Doll". The eponymous 'just your doll' underpinned by reverberant bass hits and rapid hi-hats all combine seamlessly. Similarly, on the chorus of Just A Hint !SM, the "Just a hint // Give me all of it" is impossibly catchy, again building over rapid percussion and fuzzy kicks. My favorite part comes later in the verse - "You make it harder to think // I wanna come to my senses", or even better - "I want to introduce my // separate // personalities" the rhythm in her voice under 'separate personalities' is exceptional. Right at the end of that line, the percussion pauses for just a second, before dropping back into the melody. Just A Hint was one of the first Snow Strippers songs I came to truly love, as its infectious energy has indelibly marked my brain. 

Beyond the quality and energy of these songs, Night Killas Vol. 1 is also extremely rich with personality. It is unmistakably a Snow Strippers project, due to both Graham's excellent production and Tati's strong vocal performances. Comin Down !SM is a perfect example of both - the modulated "Light it up // Keep me high", exhaling instrumental, and signature laughter sample all show the EP's DNA transparently, which I appreciate both as a fan and a critic. Although it does not run the full gamut like So What If I'm A Freak, these idiosyncratic details build upon the relationship I have to the duo. Later, in the verse, I love Tati's passionate delivery of "Cold realities // Will you ever answer me?". Of course - this is scaffolded by the production, the paired kicks under each syllable of 'ever'; the percussion dropping out entirely under 'answer me?' - it's exactly the vocal/instrumental synergy that has made me a fan of both halves of the duo. Or, on Cautious, where the echoing fricatives and plosive of her syllables fill the space of percussion. Like on Stab The Voice, where the end of "voice" was repeated, here we get the repeated S-sound at the end of "Cautious" to be echoed and panned, again feeling like a high-hat. Across both these tracks, her unique voice atop signature production makes the duo's style hard to escape: more to love for old fans, easy favorites for new ones.  

When they're not being chopped or sped up, the vocals are even stronger, with rich lyrical themes. Though at times their lyrics are a bit base or hedonistic, there's undeniably some effective passages here. On the aforementioned Just Your Doll - "It's like you can't decide // Searing burns // I hate feeling you like". I appreciate how they wrestle with this sort of limerence in a relationship, almost a stockholm syndrome: "I am just your doll, I promise // I'm lost in hell". There's a sort of numbness, an unhappy submission, which is very compelling. Again, the earlier Comin Down: "Let me choke // Keep me tied up on the ground // In a haze", and of course the repeated "Only hate myself". Very visceral imagery - perhaps it's not the drugs keeping her high, it's the toxic yet intoxicating love of the messy relationship. Although these ideas are not exhaustively explored, I argue they don't need to be. For songs that are upbeat and danceable, it is inherently engaging to contrast their energetic disposition against bleak lyrics. It speaks to the intrinsic conflict - it is, at times, energizing and fun to be self-destructive. Of course, this fun is short-lived, and eventually results in the choking self-hatred that's gestured toward. To have these themes loosely dissected over amazing beats is all I can ask for. 

Considering Night Killas Vol. 1 as a whole, I think it is, on average, their strongest collection of songs. With a clean focus on catchy, danceable tracks, it's hard to think of much criticism at all. At a short sixteen minute runtime, it doesn't come close to wearing thin, instead it leaves me hungry for more. Although Lacerate might be a better standalone track, and Under Your Spell has more plays than everything here combined, I can't help but love the songs here. When I want to dance alone in my apartment at night to some easy-listening electroclash, this EP is what I'm reaching for.
