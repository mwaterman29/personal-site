♡

Jane Remover

As I wrote in my 2025 year-end list, it seems like Jane's winstreak never ends. Regardless of the genre, alias, collaborator, planetary alignment, or the way the wind blows, she consistently puts out amazing music. Following 2023's Census Designated, she released a handful of singles, which I originally I thought would belong to her newest album. Well past Revengeseekerz release, and these tracks were still orphaned. Finally, over a year after their original release, Flash in the Pan and its peers are no longer orphaned - grouped into an EP, called "♡".  Though its overarching narrative, consistent sound palette, and internal progression are all looser than her full albums, it's still an outstanding collection of tracks. Even if the whole was no better than the sum of its parts, there's a number of damn good parts to talk about. Through the tracks on ♡, Jane explores an alt-pop and R&B sound, blending her signature glitchy, dense production with easy listening jams, to great effect. 

Sonically, this is another new direction in Jane's ever-growing repertoire. First and foremost, I appreciate the focus on Jane's vocals over her production. Though her vocals are always great - they're often drowning in effects, chopped and overtaken by the production, or more recently, spoken or rapped instead of sung. Here - there's an appreciable focus on her delivery and tone, amplifying the details of her voice. On So What? !SM, I love that her vocals are forward and clear. Leading into the chorus, I adore the rising "a girl like me", floating above hummed layers below it. Even the vibrato and extended delivery of the single word "alone" during the chorus has me coming back to this song again and again. Of course, this doesn't escape her production style - instead, it's amplified by it. The choppy "I-I-I-I" emphasizes the vocals, and effectively bridges the gap between the clean vocals with the rapid drums, booming bass, and variety of samples that wouldn't appear in a standard R&B track. Truly an exceptional track, and I'm glad it wasn't lost to her archives. The clear, bright vocals continue on How To Teleport !SM, her falsetto cleanly placed above the many layers of percussion. I appreciate the contrast here - there's almost no voice modulation, a welcome contrast to the dense web of drums, shakers, and hi-hats. With short verses, repetitive choruses, and many rhythms to catch, How To Teleport is a fantastic dance track. Where Census Designated has me solemly headbanging, and Revengeseekerz makes me want to mosh, these tracks just get me grooving. 

In addition to the sound, with this new direction comes new lyricism, which is fantastic throughout the EP. Where Revengeseekerz is often bombastic, aura-farming, even conceited, many tracks here are wistful, love-drunk, and poignant. On Magic I Want U !SM - I particularly like the dual use of gold. First - "There's gold in your mouth, what you say about me // There's gold in my mouth, we make magic". Clever, a bit tongue-in-cheek (literally), and very effective. Where Imogen Heap's Loose Ends illustrates "kissing without kissing", I think this might be kissing with... kissing. This continues later in the verses with "it's gold that he can't hit the stage without me" - meaning rich or ironic, rather than the "magic" it meant earlier. Similarly, on Flash in the Pan !SM, I love the back-and-forth that the track develops. First, he says to her: "I'm on stage and you're thinkin' about other guys in front of me?". Then of course, later, "I'm on stage and he's thinkin' 'bout other girls in front of me". Again, she paints the duality, the back-and-forth, with clever lyricism. The chorus is also fantastic - in large part due to the clarity and quality of her vocals. On Flash In The Pan, I adore the rising, airy delivery of "I'm trashy for a bitch with no credentials"; the chopped "P-p-pretty boy said I was just a flash in the pan" atop the muddied low end and glittering percussion. Often, bass sounding "muddy" is a negative: lacking definition or resolution. Here, I mean it in praise: it feels uncertain and somber, and provides a great contrast to her voice. 

Though these songs excel on their own, they're even better in the context of Revengeseekerz' release. It has many of the same ideas and lyrics, but with a completely different palette, demonstrating Jane's impressive range. Again I return to So What? - as the line "If I'm not dancing all night, then I might just leave" appears directly on TURN UP OR DIE - except here, it's not followed by "fuck it, distort my spine". Later - "I put down the mic just to feel that way forever". This same line lands on Fadeoutz - but with a lot more weight behind it. I could go on - So What is nearly ubiquitous [in her recent work](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TWSsdp_yG54). It's not just so what - on Music Baby, the "Please god forgive me, I just wanna party" is an obvious match for Dreamflasher's "Please god save me, I'm so turnt right now." Though many of the ideas and lines ended up working better under the noisy, chaotic, overwhelming sonic umbrella of Revengeseekerz, this EP is refreshingly clear. 

This EP doesn't just parallel the sounds of Revengeseekerz, but completes the ideas. Though the throughline is a bit hazy, it's obvious that the songs follow a secretive romance with another musician - one who might not be willing to be open about dating someone who's trans. On Revengeseekerz' Experimental Skin, she screams "Turn you away from your friends cuz I turn you on". Here, this idea is expanded upon, giving more color to the situation. On the bridge of Music Baby !SM, she asks "Are you gonna let your feelings show when they turn back on the lights?". Continuing into the chorus - she repeats "I don't give a fuck what they think about us // We just make music baby". I find it really compelling that, throughout the EP - the focus is on love, music, and love for music - rather than whether people accept this love. Comparing to the explosive and dynamic songs of her latest album, slower songs like Dream Sequence !SM are a lovely change. It's refreshing to see the tender side of her: "I wanna take it serious // And take it slow // Said, 'I wanna make it to Christmas' // But I fall off the bone". The impassioned delivery over the burning, wailing guitars is extremely compelling. It's not explosive, raucously dynamic, or even particularly danceable; it's direct, heavy, and deeply moving. It feels like Jane understands music - her vocals, her guitars, her percussion - to an impossible degree. Her ability to build an impactful track cannot be overstated - whether that's emotionally moving, making you dance, or changing the sound of yet another genre, forever.

At just six tracks. The tracks here retain everything I love about Jane's music: intricately dense production, energy and danceability, and signature glitchy, digital flare - while applying it to a new sound. At this point, it feels like there's no genre she *couldn't* excel in. Through her work as Jane Remover, aliases, and even the deleted Indie Rock, her creative output is nearly unparalleled. For me, the deeper I engage with her work, the more there is to love. I can only hope that easy-listening songs like these help another million listeners fall down the rabbit hole of her work, which has been so rewarding as a long-time listener and fan. I truly can't wait to see what she makes next.
