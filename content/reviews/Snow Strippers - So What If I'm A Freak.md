So What If I'm A Freak
Snow Strippers

Earlier this year, I finally saw Snow Strippers live, redoubling my enthusiasm for their idiosyncratic flavor of electroclash. In the months since, I've felt a renewed obsession with their music, accompanying my morning commutes, daily grind, nightly workouts, and late-night listens. It's no secret that I'm a huge fan of modern electroclash, and Snow Strippers are an important facet not only of my taste, but of the overall landscape of neo-electroclash. To celebrate that, this April, I'll be sampling their highlights for a number of reviews. Where better to start than with a surprisingly accessible, obviously catchy, easily danceable track - So What If I'm A Freak !SM. 

This song, more than any other in their discography, feels optimized for being catchy. The instrumental elements here all complement each other, building a straightforward and enjoyable rhythm. Straightforward doesn't mean boring, though - the opening keyboard, light percussion, and oscillating bass always make me want to dance. Building on the instrumental, Tati's voice is smooth and pleasant, often serving more as a melody than a verse, with the lyrics chopped into "Push onto my-" and "Sometimes I wonder if-", layered to fill the space. I've always found chopped vocals to be like hooks in my brain. Something about unfinished words or sentences - even just fragments of an earlier verse - keeps me engaged, this song included. Nothing is out of place; every second is plainly enjoyable to listen to. Often, I find that otherwise well-arranged tracks either wear thin as the track fails to evolve, or introduce components that don't fit, feeling discordant or jarring. Here, that's not the case - every element works seamlessly together, and has left me looping this song for hours at a time. 

Beyond the instrumental composition, the sample selection is a key addition to this track. Even conceding the enjoyable sound of the track, I could understand that, to some, this song may feel like a caricature - primarily because of the cartoonish array of samples that accompany the beat. Although it might seem outlandish, I think the ironic disposition actually serves the song well. As a listener, it's clear that we're not supposed to take it overly seriously - and the cue to just have fun is a good one. The combination of stock-sounding gunshot samples, movie-villain laughter, and "Aw, yeah!" all work well over the beat. And of course, leading into the drop with "Fun fact, if I actually killed myself, you could get arrested for that!" is so absurd I can't help but love it. Each one adds to the carefree, fun listening experience, and really differentiates this track from other easy-listening electroclash.

Sure, on first listen, these samples might seem out of place, unfitting, or even corny - but they're precisely why the song is so addictive, and why it grew on me the same way it has for many others. The unexpected result of an amazing track made up of these disparate parts is really special. There's a clear focus on picking euphonic samples - the opening "you could get arrested for that" is not chosen purely because it's funny, it's chosen because it's quite rhythmic, and is a perfect foil for the piano leading into the drop. The same is true for the split "Ah - yeah!", "Keep it- // Keep it coming" and text-to-speech "bruh" - all serve a dual purpose. On the surface, they serve as a tagline or signature, as touchpoints for existing fans, or gateways for new ones. Beyond that, though, they punctuate the track and sound great doing so. I think this principle is clearest with the gun samples on this track. Consider, for comparison, JPEGMAFIA's [REBOUND!](https://open.spotify.com/track/7J8V76XTLhJXAm8hwCQKXG?si=b09a4fe017654d0b), where you can even hear the shell casing. This true-to-life gunshot furthers the narrative - "I plan on confronting you //  Show up in class, I ain't packing no lunchable". Here, it's exactly the opposite. The unrealistic gun loading isn't about aura or violence, it's comic relief, shifting to fill a percussive role, not a ballistic one. This diverse collection really makes this track the right gateway into Snow Strippers' discography: catchy, engaging, loopable, while still being unmistakably their unique sound. It's easy to latch onto a funny sample before diving deeper into their more ethereal and innovative work. 

Over and over, I come back to this song, and I enjoy it more every time. Yes, it's a bit confusing on first listen. Yes, the samples are odd to say the least. But those traits are what make the song so special: they turn a unique smorgasbord into a sleeper hit. It's impressive to make a song that sounds this good. It's doubly impressive to make it out of such a patchwork of samples. So What If I'm A Freak is a fantastic track - blending clean production with odd samples to walk the line between accessible and engaging; an open door to a wider, more esoteric, and rewarding discography.

Track Ratings:
So What If I'm A Freak - 100
